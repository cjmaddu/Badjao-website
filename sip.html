PROBLEM STATEMENT

Will the Compost of vegetable and fruit peelings make the Solanum lycopersicum grow taller?

HYPOTHESIS

If we use the compost of vegetable and fruit peelings for the plant, then the Solanum lycopersicum will grow taller.

VARIABLES

INDEPENDENT VARIABLE/s

- Presence of Peelings / Compost in plant

DEPENDENT VARIABLE/s

- Growth of the plant / Height of the plant

CONSTANT / CONTROLLED VARIABLE/s

- Plant used
- Amount of Water used
- Location of the Plant
- Amount of sunlight given
- Type of Soil
- Duration of the Experiment
- Unit of measurement used



Control and Experimental Set-up

CONTROL SET-UP
Location: open space garden, warm place.
This setup will have no compost fertilizer.
The soil will be 7 inches deep, and will be watered with 1 and a half cups of water per week.

EXPERIMENTAL SET-UP
Location: open space garden, warm place.
This set-up will have vegetable and fruit peelings as compost fertilizer.
The soil will be 7 inches deep, and will be watered with 1 and a half cups of water per week.
1 cup of the compost will be added to the plant weekly. It comprises of:

- Banana Peel ; 7 pieces , 400g
- Chopped Eggplant ; 5 pieces, 200g
- Chopped Bitter Melon ; 16 pieces, 900g
- Chopped Philippine Lime ; 11 pieces, 500g
- Chopped Onion ; 4 pieces, 100g
- Chopped Potato ; 6 pieces, 100g
- Chopped Tomato ; 12 pieces, 300g
